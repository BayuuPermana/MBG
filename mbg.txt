Business Model Canvas: GiziSync (MBG Management Platform)  
Project Name: GiziSync (Sistem Manajemen & Pelaporan Gizi Nasional)  
Date: November 29, 2025  
Status: Enterprise / B2G Concept  

1. Executive Summary  
The Core Concept: A dedicated ERP (Enterprise Resource Planning) web and mobile platform tailored for the decentralized kitchens (Cabang/Dapur Umum) within the MBG program. It digitalizes daily procurement, financial reporting, and menu planning processes. Importantly, it consolidates procurement data to generate a Real-Time Food Price Map, enabling the central government to audit expenses and track regional inflation.

3. Roadmap & Next Steps  

Phase 1: The Pilot (Validating the Price Tracker)  
- Goal: Demonstrate the effectiveness of "Markup Detection."  
- Action: Deploy in one city (e.g., Solo or Bogor) with 50 kitchens.  
- Feature: Simple input asking "What did you buy?" plus "Upload Photo of Receipt."  
- Outcome: Produce a "Heatmap of Egg Prices" for that city to showcase data value to the government.  

Phase 2: The Compliance Engine (B2G Pitch)  
- Goal: Become the official tool.  
- Action: Develop an automatic SPJ (Surat Pertanggungjawaban) generator.  
- Pitch: "Using our app eliminates the need for an accountant by automatically generating PDFs for the government."  

Phase 3: The Marketplace (Supply Chain Optimization)  
- Goal: Close the loop.  
- Action: If Kitchen A requires 50kg of chicken daily and prices are high, the system connects them directly with a local farm offering lower prices.  

4. Technical Challenges & Risk Assessment  

- Fake Data Entry: Operators might input false low prices to conceal information or false high prices to embezzle funds.  
- Solution: OCR (Optical Character Recognition) on receipts requiring users to photograph receipts; AI verifies numbers and rejects mismatches.  

- Internet Connectivity: Rural kitchens may experience poor signals.  
- Solution: "Offline-First" Progressive Web App (PWA) that functions offline, stores data locally, and syncs when connectivity returns.  

- UI Complexity: Kitchen staff are cooks, not accountants.  
- Solution: Icon-based UI featuring pictures of eggs, rice, etc., with minimal typing and voice-to-text input for quantities.
